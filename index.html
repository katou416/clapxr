Clap XR uses the position of the player’s palm as the detection area, and determines whether the two sides hit each other through real-time changes in the position of the virtual hand. All UI button interactions use high-ffve mode to allow the user to adapt to the interaction mode and rhythm of the game in advance, before the game starts.


UI button interaction includes at least two modes, short trigger mode and tracking mode, which involves the dynamic changes and thinking mode of the experiencer’s body during the interaction. The short trigger mode will emphasize the reaction power and subliminal activity of the participants, and will also be more likely to produce a stronger palm collision, this trigger mode will emphasize the musical sensation and let the player enter the "ffow" state of the music game. Long trigger mode requires two players to hold a certain amount of time on a preset track for the button to move before they can trigger. This requires the player to keep their hands close to each other and follow each other’s movements, and this mode emphasizes more on observing and feeling the other person’s actions, and experiencing the physical perception of human touch.


In the game, the control of music, users and the relationship between users is an important focus of interaction design, and the method of time control is mainly used to control the process experience of the game in the game, so as to prevent the user from waiting excessively in the experience and causing the destruction of the environmental atmosphere.


The two sides of the game can’t see each other, and this mode makes the player lose the visual impact, and the sudden tactile experience will enhance the perceptual experience.


The game supports two positioning methods: ArUco code recognition and positioning, and LBE large space positioning, which synchronizes the virtual location of two players. Hand recognition is used to obtain hand joint data to track the detection area.

In the feedback from the exhibition, users expressed the emotional support that ClapXR has given them, and this convergence model has allowed them to feel connected to others and music in the experience, which has also given them a deeper understanding of the music content.

During the design process, ClapXR made many adjustments and modiffcations to the interaction mode based on user feedback. Seeing each other will lead to the lack of experience in the interaction process, which can easily lead to the game becoming boring mechanical movement, and in terms of the user’s feeling of music, a more concise interface and simple high-ffve feedback make it easier for players to immerse themselves in music. And as for the scale between people, more physical parts (shoulders, feet, head, etc.) will make the experience easy to become bad and out of balance, and high-ffve, as a simple and easy-to-understand form of interaction, is the easiest form for users to participate in the experience. For XR, it should be simple and easy to use.


ClapXR has introduced some interesting design concepts, including more diverse feedback interactions, using the other person’s physical touch as a positive feedback to not only have a novel experience, but also a sense of satisfaction with friends, making the atmosphere of the offfine experience more sufffcient. The evolution from the conventional default scene of "seeing each other" to "not seeing each other" will enhance the novelty of the experience, and the design after losing visual dependence will lead users to strengthen their dependence on visual compensation, which is reffected in the speciffc performance of this design that users will look forward to the feedback of each other’s palms more.
